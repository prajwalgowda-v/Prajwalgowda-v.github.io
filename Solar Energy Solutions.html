Solar System Components

The building blocks of your solar power system

Solar Panels

Solar panels are the most visible part of the solar system. They're installed on the rooftop, facing the South direction. When sunlight falls on these panels, they absorb that sunlight and convert it into electricity.

Solar Inverter

Without a solar inverter, the entire solar system would have been an impractical model. The solar inverter converts DC into AC, making it usable for household appliances.

Mounting Structures

These are the pillars that support solar panels. They must be of a standard grade to withstand heavy winds during storms and cyclones and hold the weight of the solar panels for 25 years.

Solar Accessories

AC and DC cables, combiner boxes, earthing strips and wires, and MC4 connectors are essential components that make the entire solar grid functional and safe.

Bi-directional Meter

Also known as a net meter, it keeps track of electricity supplied to the grid and electricity imported from the grid, ensuring accurate billing for your solar system.

Longevity

When maintained properly, an on-grid solar system can last for 25 years. Professional maintenance ensures peak efficiency throughout its lifespan.

How It Works

The journey from sunlight to electricity

1

Solar Energy Capture and DC Power Generation

Solar panels, composed of photovoltaic (PV) cells, absorb photons from sunlight and free electrons within the cell's semiconductor material. This movement of electrons generates a direct current (DC).

2

DC-to-AC Conversion via the Solar Inverter

The inverter takes the panel-generated DC and inverts it into alternating current (AC), matching the frequency (50 Hz in India) and voltage of the local grid.

3

Bidirectional Meter Integration

Once converted to grid-compliant AC, the electricity flows through a bi-directional (net) meter. This meter precisely records both incoming units drawn from the grid and outgoing units exported from your solar system.

4

Household Consumption of Solar-Generated AC

During the day, your home's appliances draw AC power directly from the inverter output. Because solar power is typically the first source used, it reduces or altogether replaces the need to import electricity from the grid.

5

Exporting Excess Solar Power

If your panels produce more electricity than your household consumes at any moment, the surplus AC automatically travels back through the bi-directional meter into the grid. Your meter records these exported units, which may earn you financial credit.

6

Importing Grid Power When Needed

After sunset or during low-sunlight periods, the bi-directional meter switches to import mode if your solar output drops below your home's demand. It draws the shortfall from the grid, using up any previously accrued credits first.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about solar energy

Does on-grid solar need a battery?

No. That's the beauty of installing an on-grid solar system. It does not require a battery connection like off-grid and hybrid solar systems, which makes it easier to maintain and the most affordable option of the three types.

Do solar panels work at night?

No. Solar panels generate electricity when photons from sunlight knock electrons loose in the PV cells. At night, an on-grid solar system draws power from the grid to keep a house running.

What is the difference between on-grid and hybrid solar systems?

The main difference is that a hybrid system is connected to the grid and batteries, while an on-grid solar system is connected only to the grid.

What is the on-grid solar panel price in India?

The prices vary based on the type of solar panels. For instance, the latest technology, MonoPERC half-cut solar panels in India, cost Rs. 24 per watt.
